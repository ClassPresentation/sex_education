Abstinence Only

Pros:

  • "Abstinence from sex is the only form of pregnancy prevention that is 100% effective. Every method of contraception has a risk of failure, however, small, but a teen who practices abstinence will never become pregnant" (Lowen).
  • "A teen who practices abstinence and is in a romantic relationship is secure in the knowledge that her/his partner is not interested in them purely for sex -- a concern of many teens" (Lowen).
  • "Abstinence costs nothing and there are no side effects as there are with oral contraceptives and many other forms of pregnancy prevention" (Lowen).

Cons:

  • Doesn't teach about mental sexual health and similar issues that occur in romantic relationships
  • “Abstinence-only education doesn't affect the rates at which teenagers decide to have sex” (Boskey).

Safe Sex Education

Pros:

  • “Admittedly, comprehensive sex education doesn't discourage kids from having sex either. However, it does teach them how to do so more safely.” (Boskey)
  • “Just Because You Have a Raincoat Doesn't Mean It's Going To Rain” (Boskey) This means that just because a person has the knowledge to have safe sex does not mean they are going to.
  • Many comprehensive sex curriculums also speak on abstinence and do not shy away from the topic, unlike abstinence curriculums that only talk about birth control as negative and ineffective.

Cons:

  • The contraceptive methods taught in comprehensive sex education are not 100% effective because only abstinence is 100% effective
  • By shining light on the topic of sex it may encourage early sexual activity
  • Teachers may not be experts on sexual education and can teach incorrect information
  • Not LGBTQ+ inclusive
